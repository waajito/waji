PLEX / CANTO

Designing and developing an entire DeFi ecosystem.
From cyberpunk terminals to Windows 95 nostalgia.

ROLE
Founding Engineer TIMELINE
2022 — 2024 STACK
Next.js, TS, Web3

I joined Plex as a founding engineer and became the sole frontend owner for Canto, a Layer 1 blockchain network. What started as "build a lending interface" turned into 2.5 years of designing and developing an entire DeFi ecosystem.

I owned everything user-facing: architecture, components, animations, performance, and the design direction that made Canto stand out.

[IMAGE: V1 TERMINAL UI]

V1 — The Cyberpunk Beginnings

MAY — SEPT 2022

We launched with nine separate applications—lending, staking, governance, DEX—each on its own subdomain. The aesthetic was aggressive: Neon scanlines, typing animations, glitch effects.

The Problem: Users had to reconnect their wallets on every subdomain. Beautiful code, terrible UX. I learned that technical elegance and user experience can be completely disconnected.

V2 — The Consolidation

SEPT 2022 — JUNE 2023

I tore everyone down and rebuilt from scratch. I consolidated nine apps into one unified platform where users never lose context. This is also when I discovered most users were on mobile.

Impact

  • Zero wallet disconnection complaints.
  • 300% increase in mobile usage.
  • Peak productivity: 157 commits in 90 days.

V3 — The Masterpiece

JULY 2023 — AUG 2024

The big design decision: Windows 95 aesthetic. Not just for nostalgia, but because it represented a moment when computers felt magical. Functional yet delightful.

[IMAGE: V3 WIN95 DESKTOP UI]

Technical Evolution

We migrated from Vite to Next.js for better SSR performance. I implemented full TypeScript adoption and a custom animation framework. The result was 40% faster load times and an interface that users shared purely for its vibes.

Learnings

Unique Identity Wins. In a sea of generic DeFi clones, our distinct aesthetics (Cyberpunk then Retro) created a cult following.

Constraints Breed Creativity. Mobile requirements forced us to be intentional with every pixel.
