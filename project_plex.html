I joined Plex as a founding engineer and became the sole frontend owner for Canto. What started as "build a lending interface" turned into 2.5 years of designing and developing an entire DeFi ecosystem. I owned everything user-facing: architecture, components, animations, performance, and the design direction.

V1 — THE CYBERPUNK ERA

May – September 2022. We launched with nine separate applications—lending, staking, governance, DEX—each on its own subdomain. The aesthetic was aggressive: Neon scanlines, typing animations, glitch effects.

[V1_TERMINAL_SCREENSHOT]

The Problem: Users had to reconnect their wallets on every subdomain. Beautiful code, terrible UX.

V2 — CONSOLIDATION

I tore everything down and rebuilt from scratch. I consolidated nine apps into one unified platform where users never lose context. This led to a 300% increase in mobile usage and zero wallet disconnection complaints.

V3 — THE MASTERPIECE

The final version. We adopted a Windows 95 aesthetic. Not just for nostalgia—but because it felt functional yet delightful.

Technical Wins

> Migrated to Next.js for SSR.
> Full TypeScript adoption.
> 95% Test Coverage.
> 40% Faster load times.
