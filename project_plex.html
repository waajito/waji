A 2.5-year evolution from game developer to building some of the most innovative DeFi interfaces in the blockchain space. A story of evolution, persistence, beautiful failures, and technical achievement.

The Pre-Plex Foundation

Before Plex, I was a solo game developer crafting mobile games as a hobby. Unity was my playground, and I was determined to create the next big gaming sensation. Game development taught me more than just coding—it forced me to master Photoshop, Illustrator, Figma, Maya, and Blender. When you're a solo dev, you wear every hat. This multi-disciplinary background became my secret weapon in DeFi. By the time Plex found me, I had an unusual combination of technical skills, design sensibility, and product thinking.

May 2022: The Beginning

I joined Plex as a frontend engineer to work on Canto—a Layer 1 blockchain. The plan was simple: implement designs. But startups expand your role. Within weeks, I wasn't just implementing designs; I was making design decisions, optimizing user experiences, and bridging the gap between technical implementation and user-facing polish.

The Evolution: Four Phases

Phase 1: The Learning Curve (V1 Era)

The Challenge: Build a suite of DeFi applications with a cohesive visual identity.
Key Learning: Beautiful code doesn't automatically mean beautiful user experience.

V1 was where I cut my teeth on blockchain. The terminal aesthetic emerged from my Cyberpunk inspirations. Those neon scanlines weren't just flair—they were an attempt to make blockchain feel futuristic. However, our modular architecture (9 separate subdomains) was a UX nightmare. Users had to reconnect wallets constantly.

[V1_TERMINAL_UI_SCREENSHOT]

Phase 2: The Consolidation Master (V2 Era)

The Challenge: Fix V1's UX flaws without compromising the vision.
Key Learning: Sometimes you have to tear everything down to build something better.

I rebuilt the platform to solve the wallet connection problem—a product-killing flaw. This phase pushed me beyond frontend; I optimized state management, designed cross-chain flows, and focused on mobile-first design. Peak Performance: 157 commits in 90 days.

Phase 3: The Lost Masterpiece (Carthage Era)

Calculated component architecture and mature design systems. Although cancelled, Carthage taught me that the value isn't just in shipping, but in becoming the kind of developer who can build something great.

Phase 4: The Masterpiece (V3 Era)

The Challenge: Create the definitive statement of what DeFi interfaces could be.
Key Learning: Creative expression and technical excellence aren't mutually exclusive.

[V3_WINDOWS95_AESTHETIC]

We adopted a Windows 95 aesthetic. It wasn't just nostalgia—it was a philosophy to make complex financial interactions feel approachable and delightful. Technical Evolution: From basic React to Next.js with TypeScript and comprehensive testing. 1100+ commits across the journey.

The Legacy

By August 2024, my journey with Plex was complete. I had proven that a developer with design sensibilities could build world-class user experiences in blockchain. We proved that DeFi interfaces didn't have to be ugly, intimidating, or sterile. They could be beautiful, approachable, and delightful.
